Steve Jobs

1955-2011

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American businessman, inventor, and investor best known for co-founding the technology company Apple Inc. Jobs was also the founder of NeXT and chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar. He was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Time-line of steve's life

  • 1972 - Jobs enrolled at Reed College but dropped out after a semester and later took a job working for Atari.
  • 1976 - Wozniak and Jobs showed the first board for the Apple I at the Homebrew Computer Club and incorporated as Apple Computer.
  • 1977 - Apple Computer introduced the Apple II at the West Coast Computer Fair.
  • 1980 - Apple Computer entered the stock market, making Jobs remarkably wealthy.
  • 1984 - Macintosh was introduced at the Apple Shareholders meeting
  • 1985 - After a coup at Apple, Jobs founded NeXT and later purchased the computer animation division of George Lucas’ ILM, incorporating it as Pixar.
  • 1996 - Apple purchased the NeXT operating system and brought Jobs back onboard as an advisor, and he returned to his former role as CEO in 1997.
  • 2001 - Apple released the first iPod, which changed the company and technology.
